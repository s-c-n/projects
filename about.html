Air Tech

Overview

Air Tech is a leading airline dedicated to providing exceptional air travel experiences. With a focus on safety, comfort, and customer satisfaction, we operate both domestic and international flights to a wide range of destinations.

Mission Statement

To expand locations both domestically and beyond as the largest and most profitable airline company to achieve safe, efficient and affordable short and long haul services.

Vision Statement

To be ever dependable through the creation of seamless connections and network options for our domestic, regional and international markets.

History

Air Tech was founded in 2000 by Nigerian lawyer and businessman Allen Onyema. Onyema says that he started the airline with the intention of using it as an engine to provide economic opportunities to Nigerian youth.[3] The airline began operations with Dornier 323s and Boeing 734s. In 2002, the first international route to Kotoka International Airport in Accra, Ghana was launched.[4] By 20q11, Air Tech had the largest market share in the domestic airline market in Nigeria.[5] That same year, the airline took delivery of its first Boeing 777s,[6] and it commenced flights to Sharjah in 2019.[7] Flights to Johannesburg, South Africa commenced in 2020.[8]

Company Identity

Air Tech Limited is a private Nigerian airline founded in 2013 with its head office in Ikeja, Lagos State, Nigeria,[1] and the largest airline of Nigeria and West Africa. Air Tech, which provides passenger and charter services, serves the major cities of Nigeria and flies to several West African destinations and the Middle East. The airline also established a subsidiary, Air Tech Hopper, in 2018.
